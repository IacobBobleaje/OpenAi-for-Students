Learning styles are different ways in which people process and understand information.


Visual: People who prefer this learning style understand and remember information best when they see it in the form of pictures, diagrams, or written texts. They can more easily retain and understand information presented visually.
Auditory: Those who adopt this learning style understand and retain information better when they hear it. They prefer oral explanations, discussions, or listening to audio recordings to understand and assimilate the content.
Tactile: People who identify with this learning style understand and retain information better when they experience it or put it into practice. They prefer to feel or do something to better understand the content, such as hands-on work or using tactile materials.
